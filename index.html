From Our Blog

The Journey of Milk: From Udder to Shelf

With automated milking systems (AMS), cows voluntarily walk into milking units where machines gently collect milk, record yield, and sanitize equipment. The process is not only efficient but also more comfortable for cows, as it's less stressful and happens on their own schedule.

Inside the Life of a Modern Sheep Farm:

Every day on a sheep farm begins with routine care: checking the flock, feeding, watering, and walking the pastures. Seasonal tasks like shearing, lambing, or rotating grazing areas also shape the daily rhythm. Sheep are social animals — calm, intelligent, and responsive to gentle handling — which makes building trust a big part of the job.

A Day on the Goat Farm

Every day starts with feeding and checking on the herd. Goats are naturally social and full of personality, so we take time to interact with them — not just for fun, but to monitor their health and behavior Some goats are milked daily using hygienic, low-stress techniques. Others graze and browse in rotational pastures to maintain land health and avoid overgrazing.

What Is Country Chicken?

Country chicken refers to native or indigenous breeds raised in a natural, open environment. Unlike commercial broilers that grow in controlled environments and reach market size in a few weeks, country chickens grow slowly, feed on grains and insects, and roam freely.
